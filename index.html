Xinyi District · 15 min from hotel by MRT Blue Line

Today's Route

2:00 AM

Breakfast near Ximending
Traditional Taiwanese breakfast — soy milk (豆漿) with fried dough, or egg crepe (蛋餅). Then MRT Blue Line: Ximen → Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (4 stops, ~12 min).
1
9:00 AM · ~1 hr · Free
44 South Military Village
Former military village now a cultural park. The low wooden Japanese-era houses frame Taipei 101 dramatically behind them — one of the city's best contrast photos. Opens Tue–Sun 9 AM.
📸 Stand near the entrance facing southeast for the 101 backdrop shot.
2
10:15 AM · ~1.5 hrs
Taipei 101 Observatory
Walk 5 min from the Village. 89F indoor + 91F outdoor decks with 360° city views. See the famous 660-tonne damper ball inside.
🎟️ ~TWD 600/person · Buy tickets online at taipei-101.com.tw to skip queues.
3
12:00 PM · ~30 min
Rainbow Crosswalk — Taipei City Hall
10 min walk north. Rainbow-painted pedestrian crossings — iconic photo spot, especially in the morning light.
4
12:30 PM · ~2 hrs
Xinyi District — Lunch & Explore
Malls, rooftop gardens, cafés, and street food all within the Taipei 101 block. Grab lunch here and browse at your own pace.
🍶
6:30 PM · Dinner
Takemura Izakaya
Japanese-style bar food — yakitori, cold beer, edamame. A great way to celebrate Day 1. Reserve a table if possible.
🌙 Early bedtime recommended — North Coast tour leaves at 7:30 AM tomorrow.

Sunday, June 8 FIXED TOUR

North Coast Tour

7:30 AM – 7:30 PM · Shifen · Jiufen · Yehliu

Fixed tour — confirm pickup with operator. Bring: cash, rain jacket, comfortable shoes.

Tour Route — Northeast Coast

↑
7:30 AM
Depart Hotel
Meet your guide at the agreed pickup point. Have breakfast first — it's a full 12-hour day.
1
~9:30 AM · ~1.5 hrs
Shifen — Sky Lantern + Waterfall
Write wishes on a sky lantern and release it over the mountain valley. Then walk to Shifen Waterfall — Taiwan's widest cascade, often called the "mini-Niagara."
🏮 Lantern launch ~TWD 200 · Waterfall opens 9 AM.
2
~12:00 PM · ~2 hrs
Jiufen Old Street
Stone staircases, red paper lanterns, ocean views, and teahouses — the hilltop town that inspired Spirited Away. Great souvenir shopping and local snacks (taro balls, fish balls).
🛍️ Cheaper souvenirs here than in Taipei city.
3
~3:00 PM · ~30 min
Golden Waterfall
Pyrite-rich mine runoff stains the rocks a golden-orange. Roadside viewpoint — no hiking needed. Stunning color contrast against the green hills.
4
~3:30 PM · ~45 min
Yin Yang Sea + 13 Levels Ruins
The bay's half-blue, half-orange water from copper mine drainage is a surreal natural sight. Nearby, the abandoned smelter ruins cling dramatically to the hillside above.
5
~4:45 PM · ~1 hr
Yehliu Geopark
Otherworldly coastline full of mushroom rocks and the iconic Queen's Head formation. Walk the 1.7 km coastal trail.
🎟️ Entry ~TWD 120 · Stay with the tour group for timing.
↩
7:30 PM
Return to Taipei
Tour drops you at the hotel area. Grab a relaxed dinner at Ximending.

Monday, June 9 DIY

Yangmingshan + Tamsui

Volcanic highlands in the morning · Riverside sunset town in the afternoon

Today's Route

↑
6:30 AM · Early start
Depart — MRT to Jiantan, then Bus 108
MRT Red Line → Jiantan station, then Bus 108 up the mountain (~40 min to Qingtiangang). Must arrive before fog rolls in.
🚌 Bus 108 from Jiantan Exit 1 · Every 15–20 min · TWD 15 fare.
1
8:00 AM · ~1.5 hrs · Free
Qingtiangang Grassland
Wide highland meadow in Yangmingshan NP with free-roaming water buffalo. Easy 45-min flat loop with panoramic views of Taipei below. Go early — fog rolls in by 10 AM.
☀️ No shade on the grassland — hat, sunscreen, and water are essential.
2
10:00 AM · ~45 min · Free
Xiaoyoukeng Fumaroles (Tiaoshi Bus Stop)
Hop off at Xiaoyoukeng for active sulfur steam vents and bubbling volcanic mud — rare to see this close anywhere in Asia. Strong smell but a stunning surreal landscape.
🍽
11:30 AM · Transit + Lunch
Bus down → MRT Red Line → Tamsui
Bus back to Jiantan, MRT to Tamsui (end of line, ~40 min). Lunch along the riverfront — try A-gei (stuffed tofu with noodles), Tamsui's signature snack.
3
1:30 PM · ~1.5 hrs
Tamsui Old Street
Historic riverside boulevard with food stalls and colonial-era buildings. Visit Old Fort San Domingo for harbor views. Try: iron eggs (滷蛋) and fish crackers.
🥚 A-gei stuffed tofu ~TWD 30 each — the must-eat Tamsui snack.
4
4:00 PM · ~2.5 hrs · Stay for sunset
Tamsui Fisherman's Wharf — Lover's Bridge
Bus 26 or water taxi (~20 min) to the wharf. Walk the white arch Lover's Bridge as the sun sinks into the Taiwan Strait — one of Taiwan's most beautiful sunsets.
🌅 Sunset ~7 PM in June · Arrive by 5:30 PM for a good spot on the bridge.

Wednesday, June 11 DIY

Wanhua Heritage + Beitou Hot Springs

Morning: Walk from hotel · Afternoon: 35 min by MRT to Beitou

Today's Route — Two Areas

☀️ Morning — Wanhua & Datong (walkable from hotel)

1
9:00 AM · ~1 hr · Free · 5 min walk
Longshan Temple (龍山寺)
One of Taiwan's most spectacular and active temples — 18th-century, incense smoke curling through golden shrines, locals chanting and praying. Buddhism, Taoism, and folk religion all coexist here. Open 6 AM–10 PM. Visiting in the morning means fewer tourists and more authentic atmosphere.
🙏 Grab free temple fortune sticks (擲筊) for fun · Dress modestly, shoulders covered.
2
10:15 AM · ~30 min · Free · 2 min walk
Huaxi Street Night Market (華西街)
Right next to Longshan Temple, Huaxi (also called "Snake Alley") is one of Taiwan's oldest night markets. In the daytime it's calm and great for browsing herbal medicine stalls, traditional snacks, and local Taiwanese street food.
3
11:00 AM · ~1.5 hrs · Bus from hotel · 15 min
Dihua Old Street (迪化街)
A beautifully preserved 19th-century trading street in the Dadaocheng district — the oldest commercial area in Taipei. Baroque and Minnan-style colonial shop fronts house traditional Chinese medicine stores, dried goods merchants, tea shops, fabric markets, and now trendy cafés. Fascinating contrast of old and new.
🛒 Buy: premium teas, traditional Chinese herbs, dried fruit snacks, local rice crackers · Prices better than elsewhere.
4
12:30 PM · ~45 min · 5 min walk from Dihua
Dadaocheng Wharf (大稻埕碼頭)
A short walk from Dihua Street brings you to the riverside wharf. Beautiful views across the Danshui River, with old red-brick warehouses behind you. A peaceful lunch spot with riverside cafés and picnic-friendly benches. Great breeze off the river.

♨️ Afternoon — Beitou Hot Springs (MRT Red Line, 35 min)

🚇
1:30 PM · Transit
MRT to Xinbeitou Station
From Zhongshan MRT, take the Red Line north to Beitou, then transfer to the Xinbeitou Branch Line (1 stop). Total ~35 min from the hotel area. Simple and direct — no buses needed.
🚇 MRT fare ~TWD 40 one-way · Xinbeitou Branch Line train is separate — note the platform sign.
5
2:30 PM · ~1 hr · Free · Closed Mondays
Beitou Hot Spring Museum
Housed in a stately 1913 Japanese bathhouse building surrounded by trees and a stream. Learn the full history of Beitou's hot spring culture through well-presented exhibits. Remove shoes at the entrance (swap for sandals provided). Free entry.
🏛️ Open Tue–Sun 10 AM–6 PM · Free entry · Takes 30–45 min · A very peaceful, beautiful building.
6
3:30 PM · ~30 min · Free · 10 min walk
Thermal Valley (地熱谷)
A striking, vivid jade-green pool of naturally boiling water (~98°C) that steams dramatically in the open air. The name in Chinese means "Hell Valley" — fitting! Surrounded by trees, with steam rolling across the water's surface. Short visit but incredibly memorable. Free entry.
⚠️ Do not touch the water — it's near boiling. Stay behind the safety railings at all times.
7
4:15 PM · ~1.5 hrs · Public = Free
Hot Spring Soak (溫泉) — Public Foot Bath or Onsen
Several options along the Beitou stream: public outdoor foot-soaking pools are completely free and a great experience. Paid indoor private onsen facilities nearby for a full-body soak. The sodium bicarbonate water is known as "beauty water" — famously smooth on skin.
♨️ Public foot soak: FREE · Private onsen room: ~TWD 200–500/hr · Bring flip-flops and a small towel.

Thursday, June 12 DIY

CKS Memorial + Maokong Tea Country

Last full day · CKS Hall → Maokong Gondola → Ximending final night

Today's Route

1
8:45 AM · ~1.5 hrs · Free
Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall
MRT Blue Line → CKS Memorial Hall Station. Arrive at 9 AM for the Changing of the Guard ceremony — precise, dignified, performed in full ceremonial uniform every hour. The grand white marble hall and Liberty Square are stunning in morning light. Opens 9 AM.
⏰ Guard ceremony every hour on the hour · 9 AM is least crowded · Free entry.
🚇
10:30 AM · Transit
MRT → Taipei Zoo → Maokong Gondola
MRT Blue Line → transfer to Brown Line at Daan Station → Taipei Zoo (end). Gondola base station is outside the exit. Queue for the crystal glass-bottom cabin early.
💎 Crystal cabin = glass floor looking straight down · 1-in-4 gondolas · Worth the 15 min extra wait.
2
11:30 AM · ~1 hr
Gondola Stop 3 — Zhinan Temple
Exit at Station 3. Multi-tiered hilltop temple rising dramatically up the forested mountain — Buddhism, Taoism, and Confucianism all in one. Sweeping valley views and hundreds of hanging wish ornaments. Open 8 AM–8 PM.
🙏 The top tier platform has one of the best views in all of greater Taipei.
🍵
1:00 PM · ~1.5 hrs
Maokong Village — Tea Farms + Teahouse Lunch
Ride to the top station (Station 4). Hillsides covered in oolong and tieguanyin tea farms. Choose a teahouse terrace for a traditional gongfu tea set lunch with valley views.
🍵 Gongfu tea set with food ~TWD 300–500/person · The server walks you through the full brewing ritual.
🍦
3:00 PM · ~45 min
Maokong Yang Tai — Matcha Ice Cream
Famous dessert stop near the top station. Rich matcha soft-serve made from fresh Maokong leaves, with the tea farm terraces all around you. Also sells packaged tea — a perfect last-minute souvenir.
5
5:00 PM · ~3 hrs · Final Night
Ximending — Souvenirs + Last Dinner
Gondola down, MRT to Ximen — steps from the hotel. Spend your last evening shopping for pineapple cakes, sun cakes, tea sets, and face masks. Dinner from the night market stalls: giant crispy chicken, oyster vermicelli, bubble tea, scallion pancake. Pack tonight!
🧳 Pack bags tonight · Set two alarms · Book your 12:30 AM airport taxi in advance.
